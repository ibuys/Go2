What’s New

Go2 now has limited spotlight integration. To enable, select the checkbox marked “Make Bookmarks Available to Spotlight” in the application preferences. Go2 will create a folder named “Go2Data” in your Public folder, and will export each bookmark as a single, XML formatted text file in that directory. Unchecking the box in preferences will delete the folder, and prevent bookmarks from being indexed by Spotlight.


Enabling Spotlight integration also makes Go2 bookmarks available to popular launching applications like Quicksilver and LaunchBar. Spotlight indexing is the recommended method of launching bookmarks for collections of over 1000 bookmarks.


Also new is the option for Go2 to behave like a standard Mac application. Meaning that Go2’s coffee cup will appear in the Dock, as well as the menubar if this option is selected. This makes switching to Go2 faster using Alt-Tab.


Basic Introduction

Go2 is a everything the Finder “Connect to Server...” feature should have been.  It’s a system administrators tool, a universal bookmark manager, a place to store connection information for bookmarks that you need fast and reliable access to.


At the top of the main Go2 window there are three buttons and a search field. The first button, labeled with a plus sign, is to add a new bookmark. The second button, labeled with a minus sign, is used to remove existing bookmarks. The third field, labeled with a right facing triangle, is used to launch the connection to the highlighted bookmark.


Each of the three buttons also has a corresponding menu option and keyboard command. To add a new bookmark from the keyboard, press the key combination “⌘N”, or to add a new bookmark from the menu, click on “File” and then “New”. To remove a bookmark, highlight the bookmark and press “delete” on the keyboard, or click on “File”, and then “Remove Bookmark” in the menu. To launch the connection to a highlighted bookmark, press “Return” on the keyboard, or click on “File”, and then “Go To Bookmark”.

So far so good, right?


If you have a long list of bookmarks, you can use the search field to make it easier to find the one you are looking for. Just click into the search field, or press “⌘F”, and start typing. The search field will look for any part of the name or URL entered for a bookmark, so anything that you can remember will probably work.


The other four buttons are standard buttons for Mac applications. One red button with an “X” to close the window, one orange button with a minus sign to minimize it, and one green button with a plus sign to zoom the window to full screen. If Go2 is already full screen, the zoom button will return it to its normal size. The oval shaped button on the right hand side above the search filed is used to hide or show the toolbar.


You can also hide or show the toolbar by pressing the keyboard combination “option + command + T”, or by pressing “command + F” to both show the toolbar and place the curser into the search field.
